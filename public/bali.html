Bali, Indonesia

Island of Gods and Paradise

About Bali

Bali is a breathtaking Indonesian island known for its forested volcanic mountains, iconic rice paddies, beaches, and coral reefs. The island is home to religious sites such as cliffside Uluwatu Temple. To the south, the beachside city of Kuta has lively bars, while Seminyak, Sanur and Nusa Dua are popular resort towns. The island is also known for its yoga and meditation retreats.

Essential Information

Best Time to Visit: April to October (dry season)

Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)

Language: Indonesian, Balinese

Average Trip Cost: $50-100 per day (mid-range)

2-Day Itinerary

Day 1: Cultural Ubud & Rice Terraces

8:00 AM - Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Start your day at the famous Tegallalang Rice Terraces. Walk through the stunning emerald-green rice paddies and capture Instagram-worthy photos. Consider trying the jungle swing for an adrenaline rush!

Duration: 2 hours | Entry: 20,000 IDR (~$1.30)

10:30 AM - Sacred Monkey Forest Sanctuary

Visit the Ubud Monkey Forest, home to over 700 Balinese long-tailed monkeys. Walk through ancient temples covered in moss while monkeys play around you. Remember to secure your belongings!

Duration: 1.5 hours | Entry: 80,000 IDR (~$5)

1:00 PM - Lunch at Ubud Traditional Market

Enjoy authentic Balinese cuisine like Nasi Campur or Babi Guling. Try local warungs (small family-owned restaurants) for the most authentic experience.

Budget: 50,000-100,000 IDR (~$3-7)

3:00 PM - Tirta Empul Temple

Visit this sacred water temple famous for its holy spring water where Balinese Hindus go for purification rituals. You can participate in the cleansing ceremony wearing a sarong.

Duration: 1.5 hours | Entry: 50,000 IDR (~$3)

6:00 PM - Ubud Palace & Traditional Dance

Watch a mesmerizing Legong or Barong dance performance at Ubud Palace. The intricate costumes and gamelan music create an unforgettable cultural experience.

Duration: 1.5 hours | Ticket: 100,000 IDR (~$7)

Day 2: Beach Day & Sunset Temple

8:00 AM - Seminyak Beach

Start with a relaxing morning at Seminyak Beach. Enjoy the golden sand, rent a surfboard, or simply relax with a beachfront breakfast at one of the many cafes.

Duration: 3 hours | Free entry

12:00 PM - Beach Club Lunch

Experience Bali's famous beach club scene at Potato Head or Ku De Ta. Enjoy international cuisine, cocktails, and an infinity pool overlooking the ocean.

Budget: 200,000-500,000 IDR (~$13-33)

3:00 PM - Tanah Lot Temple

Visit one of Bali's most iconic temples, perched on a rock formation in the sea. Walk through the temple grounds and browse local handicraft shops.

Duration: 2 hours | Entry: 60,000 IDR (~$4)

6:00 PM - Sunset at Tanah Lot

Stay for the spectacular sunset when the temple is silhouetted against the orange sky. This is one of the most photographed sunsets in Bali.

Best viewing time: 6:00-6:30 PM

8:00 PM - Seafood Dinner in Jimbaran

End your trip with a romantic beachfront seafood dinner in Jimbaran Bay. Choose from fresh catches of the day and enjoy your meal with your toes in the sand.

Budget: 150,000-300,000 IDR (~$10-20)

Top Attractions

  • Uluwatu Temple: Clifftop temple with stunning ocean views and daily Kecak dance performances
  • Mount Batur: Active volcano perfect for sunrise trekking adventures
  • Nusa Penida: Island day trip featuring Angel's Billabong and Kelingking Beach
  • Tegenungan Waterfall: Accessible waterfall near Ubud perfect for swimming
  • Bali Swing: Instagram-famous jungle swings with panoramic valley views

Local Cuisine to Try

  • Nasi Goreng: Indonesian fried rice with vegetables, egg, and choice of protein
  • Babi Guling: Roasted suckling pig with spices (a Balinese specialty)
  • Satay: Grilled meat skewers served with peanut sauce
  • Gado-Gado: Vegetable salad with peanut sauce dressing
  • Es Campur: Shaved ice dessert with fruits, jelly, and sweet syrup

Travel Tips

  • Rent a scooter for easy transportation (international license required)
  • Dress modestly when visiting temples (sarongs usually provided)
  • Negotiate prices at markets - haggling is expected
  • Stay hydrated and use sunscreen - the tropical sun is strong
  • Download offline maps as WiFi can be unreliable in rural areas
  • Book accommodations in advance during peak season (July-August)
